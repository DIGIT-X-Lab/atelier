# ARRIVE — Animal Research: Reporting of In Vivo Experiments

*Encodes ARRIVE 2.0 (2020). A fast-start memory aid — confirm borderline calls against the live published checklist.*

## What it governs

**In vivo animal research** — preclinical experiments using live animals. ARRIVE 2.0 defines an "Essential 10" of items without which an animal study cannot be evaluated or reproduced.

## Highest-yield checklist items

1. **Sample size & justification** — the number of animals per group and how it was decided (a priori power calculation, not convenience). Underpowered animal studies are endemic.
2. **Randomization** — how animals were allocated to groups; unrandomized allocation invites bias (cage effects, order effects).
3. **Blinding** — were those administering treatment and, critically, those assessing outcomes blinded to group? Subjective outcomes without blinded assessment are unreliable.
4. **Inclusion / exclusion criteria** — pre-defined rules for excluding animals or data points, and every exclusion reported with reasons. Post hoc exclusion of "outliers" is a red flag.
5. **Ethics & regulatory approval** — animal-welfare/ethics committee approval and protocol number stated (integrity requirement).
6. **Experimental unit defined** — is the unit the animal, the cage, or the litter? Pseudoreplication (treating cage-mates as independent) inflates significance.
7. **Species, strain, sex, age/weight, husbandry** — reported fully enough to reproduce, and sex not silently single-sex without justification.

## Review prompts

- Is there an a priori sample-size justification, or does the *n* look like convenience?
- Were allocation and outcome assessment randomized and blinded? For subjective outcomes, was the assessor blind?
- Are all animal/data exclusions reported with pre-specified reasons, or do numbers quietly shrink between groups and analysis?
- Is the experimental unit correct, or are cage-mates treated as independent replicates?
- Is ethics/welfare approval stated?
