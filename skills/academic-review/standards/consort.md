# CONSORT — Consolidated Standards of Reporting Trials

*Encodes CONSORT 2010 (2025 update in circulation). A fast-start memory aid — confirm borderline calls against the live published checklist.*

## What it governs

**Randomized controlled trials.** The standard editors and reviewers expect for any paper reporting a randomized comparison; the whole point is to let a reader verify that randomization was done properly and its benefits weren't undone downstream.

## Highest-yield checklist items

1. **Randomization method** — how the allocation sequence was generated (a real random process, not alternation or date-of-birth). "Randomized" without a method is unverifiable.
2. **Allocation concealment** — was the upcoming assignment hidden from those enrolling participants? Its absence lets selection bias back in even with a valid sequence, and it is distinct from blinding.
3. **Blinding** — who was blinded (participants, providers, assessors, analysts), and how? Outcome-assessor blinding matters most for subjective outcomes.
4. **CONSORT flow diagram** — the participant flow from enrollment through allocation, follow-up, and analysis, with numbers and reasons for exclusion at each stage.
5. **Primary outcome pre-specified** — defined a priori (ideally in a registered protocol); watch for outcome switching between registration and report.
6. **Intention-to-treat analysis** — analyzed as randomized; per-protocol-only analysis can reintroduce bias.
7. **Sample size & pre-registration** — an a priori power calculation and a trial-registration identifier.

## Review prompts

- Exactly how was the randomization sequence generated, and was allocation concealed from enrollers?
- Does a CONSORT flow diagram account for every randomized participant through to analysis?
- Does the reported primary outcome match the registered/protocol outcome, or did it change?
- Is the analysis intention-to-treat, and does the sample size match a pre-specified power calculation?
