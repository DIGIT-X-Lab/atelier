# STROBE — Strengthening the Reporting of Observational studies in Epidemiology

*Encodes STROBE (2007). A fast-start memory aid — confirm borderline calls against the live published checklist.*

## What it governs

Observational studies — **cohort, case-control, and cross-sectional** designs. Studies where the investigator observes rather than assigns exposure. The central theme is that observational designs are vulnerable to biases an RCT controls by randomization, so the reporting must let a reader judge those biases.

## Highest-yield checklist items

1. **Confounding control** — which confounders were identified, and how were they addressed (matching, stratification, multivariable adjustment)? Unaddressed confounding is the signature weakness of observational work, and unmeasured confounding must be acknowledged.
2. **Selection bias** — how were participants selected, and could selection be related to both exposure and outcome? Case-control studies are especially exposed here (control selection).
3. **Participant flow ("funnel")** — numbers at each stage: eligible, included, analyzed, lost to follow-up. Unexplained attrition biases results.
4. **Exposure & outcome measurement** — clearly defined, and measured comparably across groups (measurement/misclassification bias).
5. **Bias direction discussed** — does the paper reason about the likely *direction* and magnitude of its biases, or just list "limitations" generically?
6. **Missing data & sensitivity** — how missingness was handled and whether conclusions are robust to it.
7. **Causal language discipline** — associations reported as associations, not causes.

## Review prompts

- What are the plausible confounders, and does the analysis actually address them? What could unmeasured confounding do to the result?
- How were participants (and, for case-control, controls) selected — could that selection create the observed association?
- Does the participant flow account for everyone eligible? Where did people drop out, and could that bias the estimate?
- Does the Discussion slip from "associated with" to "causes" or "reduces"?
