# Best Practices: DFG Sachbeihilfe (Individual Research Grant) Proposal

> Scope: DFG **Research Grants Programme / Sachbeihilfe (Einzelförderung)**. Authoritative basis: **form 54.01 "Proposal Preparation Instructions"** (version **06/26**), **form 10.20 "General Guidelines for Reviews"** (**04/26**), **form 53.200** (CV), **form 50.01** (Programme Guidelines), **form 1.91** (publication lists). The same form 54.01 governs Emmy Noether, Research Units, CRUs, and Priority Programmes too.
> Recency flags marked ⚠️. Where German and English versions diverge, **the German text legally takes precedence**.

---

## 0. The three-part anatomy

A DFG proposal is three parts (form 54.01, §A–C):
- **Part A – Proposal Data and Obligations** — entered in the **elan** portal (elan.dfg.de), not prose. Project metadata, participating persons, legal obligations, and a **summary in both German and English**.
- **Part B – Project Description** — the core scientific document (the "25-pager"). This is where reviewers spend their attention.
- **Part C – Attachments** — mandatory **one CV per applicant** (form 53.200) plus situational attachments (ethics votes, host-institution statements, letters of commitment from foreign partners, acceptance letters for in-press papers).

Submission is **only** via elan. Proposals can be submitted **at any time** (no deadlines), in **German or English**.

---

## 1. Length & formatting rules (hard limits — get these wrong and risk a formal return)

- **Total Project Description ≤ 25 pages**, split as: **Sections 1–3 ≤ 17 pages**; **Sections 4 onward ≤ 8 pages**. These two budgets are **separate** — no borrowing pages.
- **Font ≥ Arial 11 pt**, **line spacing ≥ 1.2**.
- **Publication list (section 3): font ≥ Arial 9 pt** (smaller font allowed only here).
- **Retain the provided template formatting.** Use the German or English template from elan.
- Attachments: each PDF **< 10 MB**, readable/copyable/printable (no locked security); follow DFG file-naming.
- ⚠️ Always re-download the template per submission — DFG revises forms ~twice/year (note the date code, e.g. "54.01 – 06/26").

---

## 2. Required section structure and order (Part B)

### **1. Starting point — State of the art and preliminary work**
- *New proposals:* explain **briefly and precisely** the state of the art **in direct relationship to your project**; make clear where you will make a **unique, innovative, promising contribution**; state the status of your **preliminary work**.
- **Critical rule: this must be self-contained — understandable without reading any cited literature.** Reviewers are explicitly told they are *not required to read any cited work*; the review is based **only on the proposal text**.
- Cite any number of publications (list in section 3). **Always indicate clearly whose work you refer to** — failure can constitute a breach of good research practice.
- List your publicly available preliminary work **with date of publication**; if DFG-funded, reference the relevant funding period.

### **2. Objectives and work programme**
- **2.1 Anticipated total duration** — intended duration; max initial funding usually **3 years**, then renewal.
- **2.2 Objectives** — concise research programme and **scientific objectives**. **DFG explicitly expects hypothesis-driven framing** — clear hypotheses/objectives logically derived from the state of knowledge.
- **2.3 Work programme incl. methods** — *for each applicant.* Detailed planned steps; for experimental projects, a **schedule of all planned experiments**. **"The quality of the work programme is critical to the success of a funding proposal."** Tasks **must correspond to funds requested**, and you must **justify what funding is needed and how used**. Classify methods (available / to develop / external). Quality-assurance measures welcomed.
- **2.4 Handling of research data** — mandatory DMP per DFG's checklist (data characteristics & scope; documentation & quality; storage & archiving; legal; reuse & long-term accessibility; responsibilities). **If your project generates no relevant data, explicitly say so.**
- **2.5 Relevance of sex, gender and/or diversity** — whether/how it's relevant to methods/work programme/objectives. If genuinely not relevant, state why.

### **3. Project- and subject-related list of publications**
- **Only works actually cited in sections 1–2.** Font ≥ Arial 9 pt. No total limit.
- ⚠️ **Highlight (bold) a maximum of 10 of your own most project-relevant publications.** The cap stays **10 total** even with multiple applicants. Follow **form 1.91**.

### **4. Supplementary information on the research context** (the ≤ 8-page block)
> If a topic is central to your research question, discuss it in sections 1–2 and merely cross-reference here.
- **4.1 Ethical and/or legal aspects** — risks/harm and mitigation; whether an ethics-committee statement is required. *Humans/human material/identifiable data:* selection criteria, sample-size justification, risks & precautions, informed-consent procedure (**ethics vote may be attached**). *Animals:* justify the model; animal numbers + statistical design; the **3Rs**. *Genetic resources from abroad:* **Nagoya Protocol** / ABS. *Safety-relevant:* **Dual Use Research of Concern (DURC)** + export-control law (attach a KEF statement if required). *International-cooperation risk* and *ecological sustainability* — **plausibility checks only**, not scientifically scored.
- **4.2 Employment status** per applicant. **4.3 First-time proposal data** — ⚠️ optional self-declaration; career stage then weighted favourably. **4.4 Composition of the project group** (people not paid from project funds). **4.5 Cooperation in Germany.** **4.6 Cooperation abroad** (signed letter of commitment attached). **4.7 Past-3-years collaborators** (conflict-of-interest). **4.8 Commercial enterprises (cooperation).** **4.9 Participation in commercial enterprises.** **4.10 Scientific equipment.** **4.11 Other submissions.** **4.12 Other information.**

### **5. Requested modules/funds**
- Explain **each item per applicant**. Funding is **modular**: **Basic Module** (direct project costs, project-specific staff, instrumentation) plus Temporary Positions for PIs, Replacement funding, Temporary Clinician Substitutes, Project-Specific Workshops, Mercator Fellows, Public Relations. Follow the module guidelines (e.g. form 52.01).

## 3. Part C — Attachments, CV & publication rules (frequently mishandled)

- **Mandatory:** one **academic CV per applicant** using **form 53.200** (compulsory template).
- **CV publication list — the "max 10 + max 10" rule (distinct from the proposal's highlighted-10):** **Category A (mandatory)** — peer-reviewed articles, conference contributions, anthology contributions, books: **max 10**. **Category B (optional)** — other published results (preprints, datasets, software, patents, etc.): **max 10**. Need not relate to the project; may span the whole career.
- CVs may disclose **career interruptions** so reviewers weight the record fairly.
- ⚠️ **No bibliometrics:** impact factors and h-indices **are not required and not to be considered.** Assessment is **qualitative**.
- ⚠️ **AI disclosure:** if generative AI was used for text/images, **disclose it appropriately.**

## 4. How proposals are reviewed, scored, ranked

**Three-stage separation:**
1. **Written reviewers (Gutachter)** — external subject experts; assess merit against fixed criteria; recommend on approval and on whether requested funds are appropriate.
2. **Review boards (Fachkollegien)** — elected panels; **comparatively evaluate all proposals in their field** and **prioritise** them against the available budget.
3. **Head Office / Joint Committee** — formal decision; quality-assures eligibility, review counts, ethics, consistency.

**Four main review criteria (form 10.20):** Quality of the project · Objectives and work programme · Applicants' qualifications · Work and research environment. Reviewers weigh **scientific excellence, relevance and originality**, considering **individual career stage**, and do **not** use non-scientific criteria.

**Scoring reality:** no single public numeric rubric for Sachbeihilfe; the operative mechanism is **competitive comparative prioritisation against a limited budget**. **Only ~1 in 3 proposals can be funded** — rejection is frequently a budget/competition outcome.

## 5. Section-by-section dos & don'ts (strong vs weak)

| Section | Strong | Weak (red flag) |
|---|---|---|
| **1 State of the art** | Self-contained; sharply defined gap; right key works (yours + competitors); explicit innovative contribution. | Literature review for its own sake; assumes reviewers read citations; misses obvious references; no clear gap. |
| **2.2 Objectives** | A few crisp, falsifiable hypotheses derived from §1. | Vague aims ("we will explore…"); wish-list with no hypothesis; objectives disconnected from §1. |
| **2.3 Work programme** | Concrete WPs/tasks with timeline; methods classified; **alternative strategies & risk mitigation**; every task maps to a budget item. | Generic methods boilerplate; no schedule; no fallback; budget that doesn't follow from tasks. |
| **2.4 Data handling** | Specific FAIR plan tied to actual data; repositories named; responsibilities assigned. | Empty platitudes or omission; failing to state "no relevant data". |
| **Budget (§5)** | Funds **derive cogently from the work programme**; staff levels justified by task. | Over-asking; staff with no defined role; co-applicants without complementary expertise. |
| **CV / pubs** | Qualitative, ≤10+≤10, career gaps noted, highlighted-10 genuinely relevant. | h-index/JIF padding; >10 in a category; highlighting >10. |

DFG's own tips: think the questions through and test on non-specialists; write concisely; use figures; minimise unfamiliar abbreviations; be hypothesis-driven; include alternative methods and risk management; justify personnel and budget tightly.

## 6. Reviewer's eye / red flags (quietly down-ranked)

- **Not self-contained** — argument only works if you read the cited papers.
- **Missing/wrong key references.**
- **Objectives ≠ work programme ≠ budget** — the most common substantive weakness.
- **No hypotheses** / purely descriptive "fishing expedition."
- **No risk management / no plan B.**
- **Budget over-ask** or staff/co-applicants without a defined role.
- **Over-ambition vs feasibility** with thin preliminary work.
- **Bibliometric padding** (h-index, impact factors) — explicitly disregarded, signals rule-blindness.
- **Self-plagiarism / unattributed reuse.**
- **Formatting violations** (under-size font, over length, locked PDFs).
- **Empty mandatory sections** (data handling; sex/gender/diversity; ethics).
- **Generative-AI text without disclosure.** ⚠️

---

## 7. Tone, voice & language

- **Register:** formal, precise, impersonal-academic. German research-funding convention rewards **dense, structured, claim-then-evidence** writing over salesmanship. Avoid hype adjectives.
- **Hypothesis-driven framing is culturally expected.** Lead with the scientific question, not the technique.
- **Clarity for non-specialists:** at least one reviewer may be adjacent rather than dead-centre. Define terms; use figures.
- **German vs English:** either accepted. English widens the reviewer pool and is the default in most natural-science/medical fields; German remains conventional in parts of the humanities/social sciences/law. The **German summary in Part A is mandatory regardless** of proposal language.

---

## 8. Most common reasons DFG proposals are rejected

1. **Budget/competition** — only ~⅓ funded.
2. **Weak/illogical work programme** — the explicitly "critical" element.
3. **Insufficient originality / unclear contribution.**
4. **Feasibility doubts** — over-ambition, thin preliminary work, no risk mitigation.
5. **State of the art gaps** — missing references, not self-contained.
6. **Budget not derived from the work programme.**
7. **Co-applicant/personnel roles unclear.**
8. **No hypotheses / purely descriptive.**
9. **Formal/eligibility/formatting defects.**

**On resubmission:** rejected proposals **can be revised and resubmitted and often then succeed.** Address the reviews point-by-point; attach a short **1–2 page cover letter** explaining the changes.

---

## 9. Pre-submission checklist

**Formatting** — [ ] ≤ 25 pp with §§1–3 ≤ 17 and §§4+ ≤ 8 (separate); [ ] font ≥ Arial 11 / spacing ≥ 1.2; pub list ≥ Arial 9; [ ] current elan template, formatting/numbering retained, latest form version; [ ] attachments < 10 MB, unlocked, named per protocol.

**Content** — [ ] all sections present and numbered exactly (1, 2.1–2.5, 3, 4.1–4.12, 5); [ ] §1 **self-contained**; key competitor & own works cited; [ ] clear **hypotheses** in §2.2 derived from §1; [ ] §2.3 tasks + **schedule**, methods classified, **alternatives & risk mitigation**, every task → a budget line; [ ] §2.4 **DMP** (or explicit "no relevant data"); [ ] §2.5 sex/gender/diversity genuinely addressed; [ ] whose-work attribution clear; in-press papers have acceptance letters; [ ] §3 = only cited works, **≤ 10 own highlighted**; [ ] §4 ethics/animal/Nagoya/DURC/export/risk/sustainability as applicable; ethics vote attached if required; [ ] §5 funds modular, each item justified, proportional to knowledge gain.

**Part A / C** — [ ] elan Part A complete; **German + English summaries**; [ ] one **CV (form 53.200)** per applicant; **Cat A ≤ 10, Cat B ≤ 10**; career gaps noted; **no h-index/impact factors**; [ ] **AI-use disclosed** if used ⚠️; [ ] foreign-partner letters of commitment + co-investigator declarations attached.

**Process** — [ ] ≥1 critical external proofread by a non-specialist; [ ] formal questions cleared with your DFG programme contact; [ ] reviewers judge on the **text alone** — assume nothing beyond the 25 pages is read.
